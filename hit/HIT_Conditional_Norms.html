Instructions     (Expand/Collapse)

In this HIT, you will be given the name of a social SETTING like an airplane. Then you can help think of examples of different Constraints where the BEHAVIOR might be expected, okay, or unexpected.

By "expected, okay, or unexpected" we use the following definitions.

  1. Expected: Behaviors that a certain kind of person would be predicted or required to do in this setting. There is often some kind of obligation here.
  2. Okay: Behaviors that a certain kind of person would reasonably do in this setting, but there is no obligation.
  3. Unexpected: Behaviors that may be socially or culturally taboo, forbidden, discouraged, or generally surprising and "not normal" for a certain kind of person to do in this setting.

There are examples of Constraints in the tables below. Constraints have a Category, Name, Relation, and Value. Play around with these options to understand them better.

  • You should think of the Constraints in a single table as having the word AND between them. That is, all the Constraints work together to define the conditions where the BEHAVIOR might be expected, okay, or unexpected.

  • The PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR is main person you are adding Constraints for, but you can also add Constraints for some OTHER PERSON. For example, if the BEHAVIOR is "serving drinks" and the SETTING is "airplane," the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR is often the flight attendant, and the OTHER PERSON is the passenger who is receiving the drink.

  • If the default list doesn't contain the Constraint that you need, feel free to create a new Constraint by directly typing it into the box.

Examples of Okay Behaviors

(under the following Constraints)

SETTING: airplane

  • BEHAVIOR: eat a ham sandwich is okay when...

    Constraints

    Category Name Relation Value Explanation
    1. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's role airplane role is passenger While other roles like pilots and flight assistants might not, passengers often eat complimentary snacks on a flight.
    2. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's attribute age-bracket is NOT infant Infants could choke on a sandwich.
    3. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's attribute religion is NOT Judaism Pork is not kosher in Judaism.
    4. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's attribute religion is NOT Islam Pork is haram (forbidden) in Islam.
    5. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's attribute religion is NOT Seventh Day Adventist Adventists recognize pork as unclean.

  • BEHAVIOR: chew on the tray table is Okay when...

    Constraints

    Category Name Relation Value Explanation
    1. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's attribute age-bracket is infant While it certainly is not required, infants are known to chew on things like tray tables. This would not be considered taboo for an infant to do.

SETTING: boat

  • BEHAVIOR: drink beer is Okay when...

    Constraints

    Category Name Relation Value Explanation
    1. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's attribute country is the United States of America We set the country first because this determines the drinking age.
    2. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's attribute age is greater than or equal to 21 This is the drinking age in the US.
    3. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's attribute religion is NOT Islam Muslims do not drink alcohol.
    4. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's attribute religion is NOT Jainism Jains do not drink alcohol.
    5. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's attribute religion is NOT Baháʼí Baháʼís do not drink alcohol.
    6. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's role boat role is NOT captain If this person is the captain or boat operator, they must remain sober or else they could face legal trouble for Boating Under the Influence (BUI).

Examples of Unexpected Behaviors

(under the following Constraints)

SETTING: airplane

  • BEHAVIOR: ask for a Coke is Unexpected when...

    Constraints

    Category Name Relation Value Explanation
    1. some OTHER PERSON's role airplane role is NOT flight attendant While it is normal to ask the flight attendant for a Coke, it is not normal to ask a passenger for a Coke.

  • BEHAVIOR: chew on the tray table is Unexpected when...

    Constraints

    Category Name Relation Value Explanation
    1. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's attribute age-bracket is NOT infant Once the passenger is old enough, the passenger is expected not to chew on things in the cabin.

  • BEHAVIOR: bring a weapon is Unexpected when...

    Constraints

    Category Name Relation Value Explanation
    1. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's role airplane role is NOT air marshal Except for the air marshal, no other person should be armed on an airplane.

SETTING: banquet hall

  • BEHAVIOR: eat dinner is Unexpected when...

    Constraints

    Category Name Relation Value Explanation
    1. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's attribute religion is Baháʼí Although most would be expected to eat at a banquet hall, there may be special conditions that would require a person to fast. One of these conditions is a religious holiday.
    2. environment holiday is Ala During the month of Ala, Baháʼís between ages 15-70 observe a fast.
    3. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's attribute age is greater than or equal to 15 See Explanation above.
    4. the PERSON PERFORMING BEHAVIOR's attribute age less than 70 See Explanation above.
